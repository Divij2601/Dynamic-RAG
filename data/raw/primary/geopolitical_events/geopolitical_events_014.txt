DRC EASTERN CONGO M23 CONFLICT

The Democratic Republic of Congo's eastern provinces have been the site of persistent armed conflict for three decades, but the resurgence of the M23 rebel group from 2021 onward, backed by Rwanda, has produced the worst humanitarian crisis in the region in years. The conflict has displaced millions, threatened Congolese state authority, and strained relations between Rwanda and the DRC as well as the broader Great Lakes region.

BACKGROUND: EASTERN DRC'S PERSISTENT CONFLICT

Eastern DRC — particularly North Kivu, South Kivu, and Ituri provinces — has experienced continuous armed conflict since the 1990s. The collapse of Mobutu's state, the spillover of Rwanda's 1994 genocide, the presence of Rwandan Hutu génocidaires in the FDLR group, and competition over the region's vast mineral wealth have sustained a complex ecosystem of armed groups. The DRC government in Kinshasa has never established effective control over the east. Dozens of armed groups operate in the region, financed by mineral extraction, taxation of civilians, and external support.

M23 ORIGINS AND FIRST INSURGENCY

The M23 (March 23 Movement) was formed in 2012 by former CNDP rebel fighters who had been integrated into the Congolese army (FARDC) but mutinied, claiming the government had not honored integration agreements. The group was widely documented as receiving support from Rwanda and, to a lesser extent, Uganda. M23 briefly captured Goma, North Kivu's capital, in November 2012 before being defeated by a UN-mandated intervention brigade in 2013. The group's fighters then fled into Uganda and Rwanda.

RESURGENCE FROM 2021

M23 resumed operations in late 2021, initially at a low intensity, then with rapidly escalating military activity through 2022 and 2023. The group's renewed capabilities were clearly linked to Rwandan support — a conclusion reached by UN Group of Experts reports, DRC, neighboring states, Western governments, and independent researchers through analysis of captured fighters, equipment, and satellite imagery of Rwandan military deployments near the DRC border.

Rwanda denied direct involvement, framing its security concerns around the FDLR and defending ethnic Tutsi communities. Rwanda and the DRC traded accusations, with the DRC filing cases at international courts. Relations between the two countries deteriorated severely.

FALL OF GOMA AND MAJOR M23 ADVANCES

In January 2025, M23 forces captured Goma, the largest city in eastern DRC and a major humanitarian hub, in a swift military operation that overwhelmed Congolese forces and the UN peacekeeping mission MONUSCO. The fall of Goma — a city of nearly two million people — was a major strategic and symbolic blow to the DRC government. M23 forces subsequently advanced further, capturing Bukavu in South Kivu in February 2025, extending their control over a vast swathe of eastern DRC.

These advances produced catastrophic displacement. Over six million people were internally displaced in DRC by early 2025, representing one of the world's largest displacement crises. Humanitarian access was severely restricted in areas under M23 control.

HUMANITARIAN CRISIS AND MINERAL DIMENSIONS

The conflict is deeply intertwined with mineral wealth. Eastern DRC holds vast deposits of coltan, gold, cassiterite, and cobalt — minerals critical for global technology supply chains. Armed groups including M23 derive significant income from controlling mineral extraction and trade routes. Rwanda was accused by UN experts of facilitating the export of Congolese minerals through its territory.

INTERNATIONAL AND REGIONAL RESPONSE

The African Union, East African Community (EAC), and Southern African Development Community (SADC) all attempted mediation. An EAC regional force deployed to eastern DRC achieved limited results. Western governments imposed targeted sanctions and suspended some aid to Rwanda — the United States and several European countries paused military and budgetary support. The DRC's own military capacity remained severely constrained, dependent on foreign mercenaries and allied armed groups. A sustainable political settlement remained elusive.
