RUSSIA-UKRAINE WAR: BATTLEFIELD DEVELOPMENTS 2023-2024

Following the dramatic reversals of 2022, in which Ukrainian forces liberated Kherson city and retook much of Kharkiv Oblast, the war in 2023 and 2024 settled into a grinding attritional struggle with neither side able to achieve decisive breakthrough. The front lines shifted incrementally, but the fundamental stalemate persisted.

THE FAILED UKRAINIAN COUNTEROFFENSIVE OF 2023

Ukraine's much-anticipated summer 2023 counteroffensive began in June after months of Western arms deliveries including Leopard 2 tanks and Bradley infantry fighting vehicles. Ukrainian forces attempted to breach Russian defensive lines in Zaporizhzhia Oblast with the strategic aim of cutting the land bridge between Russia and Crimea. The offensive fell far short of its objectives. Russian forces had constructed extensive layered defensive fortifications including dense minefields, dragon's teeth anti-tank obstacles, and interlocking fire positions. Ukrainian forces suffered heavy casualties and equipment losses attempting to breach these defenses. By autumn 2023, the counteroffensive was effectively called off with minimal territorial gains. Critics debated whether insufficient Western air support, delayed equipment deliveries, or inadequate training time contributed to the failure.

RUSSIAN ADVANCES AND THE FALL OF AVDIIVKA

While Ukraine struggled offensively, Russia applied sustained pressure on Ukrainian-held positions. Avdiivka, an industrial city in Donetsk Oblast with significant fortifications, became the focal point of intense fighting through late 2023 and into 2024. Russian forces made costly advances from multiple directions, and Ukrainian forces withdrew from Avdiivka in February 2024 after months of brutal urban combat. The fall of Avdiivka was a symbolic and strategic setback for Ukraine, representing the most significant Russian gain since the capture of Bakhmut in May 2023.

Throughout 2024, Russian forces maintained pressure along the Donetsk front, making incremental gains toward Pokrovsk, a key logistics hub, while Ukrainian forces faced mounting ammunition shortages as Western aid debates stalled in the US Congress.

WESTERN AID DEBATES AND THE US POLITICAL FACTOR

A critical development in 2024 was the prolonged delay in US military assistance. Congressional Republicans blocked a $60 billion Ukraine aid package for months, citing border security concerns and broader skepticism toward continued involvement. The aid package eventually passed in April 2024, but the delay had tangible effects on Ukrainian ammunition supplies and battlefield capability. The prospect of a potential second Trump presidency further complicated Ukraine's strategic planning, as Trump had stated he could end the war quickly — implying pressure on Ukraine to negotiate.

UKRAINIAN KURSK INCURSION

In August 2024, Ukraine launched a surprise cross-border incursion into Russia's Kursk Oblast, marking the first foreign military forces on Russian soil since World War II. Ukrainian forces seized a significant salient of Russian territory, capturing hundreds of square kilometers and thousands of Russian prisoners. The incursion was strategically bold, aimed at forcing Russia to divert forces from the Donetsk front and potentially gaining leverage for future negotiations. Russia responded by deploying North Korean troops to help defend Kursk — a development that internationalized the conflict and drew widespread condemnation. The Kursk operation marked a significant shift in Ukraine's strategic approach, demonstrating the capacity for offensive action while defending elsewhere.

DRONE WARFARE AND TECHNOLOGY

Both sides increasingly relied on drone warfare. Ukraine struck deep into Russian territory with long-range drones, hitting oil refineries, military infrastructure, and even Moscow suburbs. Russia bombarded Ukrainian cities and energy infrastructure with Shahed-136 kamikaze drones supplied by Iran, causing widespread civilian suffering. Drone warfare evolved rapidly, with both sides deploying first-person-view (FPV) drones for frontline interdiction at unprecedented scale.

ATTRITION AND HUMANITARIAN TOLL

By end of 2024, estimates suggested Russia had suffered over 300,000 casualties including killed and wounded, while Ukraine's losses were also severe though less publicly disclosed. Millions of Ukrainians remained displaced internally or as refugees in Europe. The war continued to drain European and American resources while reshaping alliances, energy markets, and global arms industries.
