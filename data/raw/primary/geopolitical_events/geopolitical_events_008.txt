YEMEN HOUTHI CONFLICT AND SAUDI INTERVENTION

Yemen's civil war, which drew in a Saudi-led coalition beginning in 2015, represents one of the world's most prolonged and devastating conflicts, creating what the UN described as among the worst humanitarian crises globally. The conflict involves a complex web of local, regional, and international actors, with the Iran-backed Houthi movement central to its dynamics.

BACKGROUND AND ROOTS OF CONFLICT

Yemen's instability is rooted in decades of political fragmentation, tribal conflicts, economic underdevelopment, and separatist tensions. The 2011 Arab Spring protests ousted longtime president Ali Abdullah Saleh. A UN-brokered transition created the Government of National Unity under Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi. The Houthis — formally Ansar Allah, a Zaidi Shia movement based in northern Yemen — had fought six wars against Saleh's government between 2004 and 2010. Exploiting the post-2011 power vacuum, the Houthis expanded from their northern Saada stronghold southward.

In 2014-2015, Houthi forces swept through Sanaa and advanced toward Aden. Hadi fled to Saudi Arabia. In March 2015, Saudi Arabia formed a coalition with the UAE and other Arab states and launched air operations against Houthi forces, aiming to restore the internationally recognized government.

SAUDI COALITION INTERVENTION

The Saudi-led coalition's air campaign proved unable to deliver a decisive military outcome despite years of intensive bombing. The coalition imposed a naval and air blockade that severely restricted food and medicine imports to Yemen, exacerbating famine conditions alongside the fighting. Airstrikes struck civilian infrastructure including hospitals, markets, wedding ceremonies, and school buses, generating intense international criticism and allegations of war crimes. Saudi Arabia and the UAE argued these strikes targeted legitimate military objectives.

UAE forces conducted effective ground operations in southern Yemen, capturing Aden and working with local allies — including the Southern Transitional Council (STC), a southern separatist movement that complicated the coalition's political objectives. The STC's aspirations for southern independence created a split within the nominally anti-Houthi coalition.

IRAN'S ROLE AND HOUTHI CAPABILITIES

Iran provided the Houthis with weapons, training, and technical expertise, transforming a relatively limited guerrilla force into one capable of ballistic missile strikes against Saudi Arabian cities and oil infrastructure. Houthi drone and missile attacks struck Riyadh and Abu Dhabi and, in September 2019, caused major damage to Saudi Aramco's Abqaiq oil processing facility — temporarily cutting Saudi oil output by half and sending global oil prices sharply higher. The sophistication of these attacks demonstrated the extent of Iranian military support.

STOCKHOLM AGREEMENT, CEASEFIRE, AND PEACE PROCESS

A UN-brokered Stockholm Agreement in December 2018 provided for a ceasefire in the key port city of Hodeidah, through which the majority of Yemen's food imports passed. Implementation was incomplete but the ceasefire in Hodeidah largely held. A nationwide ceasefire brokered by the UN took effect in April 2022 and was extended through 2022, representing the most significant reduction in violence in years. Talks between Saudi Arabia and the Houthis, facilitated by Oman and China, progressed toward a diplomatic framework, including a Saudi-Iran deal brokered by China in March 2023 that sought to normalize bilateral relations and created space for Yemen negotiations.

HOUTHI RED SEA ATTACKS 2023-2024

The October 2023 outbreak of the Israel-Gaza war injected new dynamics. The Houthis launched missiles and drones at Israel and began attacking commercial shipping in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden in solidarity with Palestinians in Gaza. This campaign caused major disruptions to global shipping, forcing vessels to reroute around Africa's Cape of Good Hope. The United States and United Kingdom launched military strikes on Houthi targets in Yemen in response, without significantly degrading Houthi capabilities. The conflict thus became intertwined with the broader regional tensions of the Gaza war, elevating Yemen's strategic significance while complicating peace efforts.
