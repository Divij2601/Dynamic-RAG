SITUATION REPORT: GAZA HUMANITARIAN CRISIS — ONGOING ASSESSMENT

CLASSIFICATION: OPEN SOURCE HUMANITARIAN INTELLIGENCE
DATE: FEBRUARY 2025
REGION: GAZA STRIP, LEVANT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Gaza Strip continues to experience one of the most severe humanitarian emergencies of the twenty-first century. Following the Hamas-led attack on Israel on October 7, 2023, the subsequent Israeli military campaign has killed over 45,000 Palestinians according to Gaza health ministry figures, with the majority of casualties being civilians including women and children. The enclave's infrastructure — hospitals, water systems, schools, and housing — has been destroyed or severely damaged. International humanitarian agencies warn of famine conditions across large portions of the territory, and disease outbreaks including hepatitis and cholera have been documented. Despite intermittent ceasefire negotiations mediated by Qatar, Egypt, and the United States, durable access for humanitarian assistance remains highly constrained.

HUMANITARIAN CONDITIONS

Food insecurity in Gaza has reached catastrophic levels classified under the Integrated Food Security Phase Classification as Phase 5 — famine — in northern Gaza and Phase 4 in the south. The World Food Programme estimates that more than 90 percent of the population is experiencing acute food insecurity. Aid delivery has been systematically hampered by the closure of crossings, restrictions on aid trucks, looting at distribution points, and active hostilities that make movement of aid personnel extremely dangerous. The UNRWA, the principal UN agency serving Palestinian refugees, faced suspension of funding from the United States and several European donors following allegations that staff members participated in the October 7 attack — allegations that UNRWA partially acknowledged while noting the broader staffing crisis this created.

MILITARY AND SECURITY SITUATION

Israeli military operations have shifted focus from northern Gaza, declared largely cleared of Hamas military infrastructure, to Rafah in the south and the central areas of Khan Younis and Deir al-Balah. Ground operations have been intense, with significant losses on both sides. Hamas continues to conduct rocket fire, though at sharply reduced capacity, and employs tunnel networks and improvised explosive devices to inflict casualties on Israeli forces. The hostage situation remains a central political constraint on Israeli decision-making; approximately 100 hostages remain in Gaza as of early 2025, with around 30 believed to be alive. Prisoner exchange negotiations have produced several partial releases but no comprehensive deal.

INTERNATIONAL RESPONSE AND DIPLOMACY

The International Court of Justice issued provisional measures in January 2024 ordering Israel to take steps to prevent genocide under the Genocide Convention, following a case brought by South Africa. Israel has contested the legal basis but faces growing international isolation. The United Nations General Assembly has passed multiple resolutions calling for a ceasefire, though these are non-binding. Several European countries, including Spain, Ireland, and Norway, formally recognized Palestine as a state in 2024. The United States remains Israel's principal military and diplomatic supporter, supplying munitions and using its Security Council veto to block ceasefire resolutions, though Washington has expressed frustration with civilian casualty levels. Arab states including Jordan and Egypt have maintained public pressure on Israel while managing domestic political implications of the conflict.

INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT AND OUTLOOK

Gaza's humanitarian trajectory continues to deteriorate absent a sustained ceasefire and open humanitarian corridors. The risk of permanent depopulation of northern Gaza is assessed as elevated given the destruction of basic services. Regionally, the conflict has energized militant groups affiliated with the Iranian-backed Axis of Resistance, contributed to Houthi shipping attacks in the Red Sea, and elevated anti-Western sentiment across the Global South. A two-state solution framework, long the stated goal of international diplomacy, appears more remote than at any previous point. The political sustainability of the Israeli war cabinet is a key variable, as coalition dynamics and domestic protests may affect operational tempo. Long-term reconstruction costs are estimated at over 50 billion dollars, with no current funding mechanism.
