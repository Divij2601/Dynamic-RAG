SITUATION REPORT: HOUTHI ATTACKS ON MERCHANT SHIPPING

CLASSIFICATION: MARITIME SECURITY SITUATION REPORT
DATE: ONGOING — 2024-2025
REGION: RED SEA, GULF OF ADEN, BABS-EL-MANDEB STRAIT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Houthi movement of Yemen — formally known as Ansar Allah — has conducted over 100 documented attacks on commercial vessels in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden since November 19, 2023, when the group seized the Israeli-linked cargo vessel Galaxy Leader. The campaign represents the most sustained threat to freedom of navigation in international waters since the era of Somali piracy. Major shipping companies including Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, and others have diverted their fleets away from the Suez Canal route to the longer Cape of Good Hope route around southern Africa. The military response by the United States, United Kingdom, and allied navies through Operation Prosperity Guardian has degraded but not eliminated Houthi attack capabilities, which have continued with Iranian logistical and intelligence support.

ATTACK METHODOLOGY AND TARGETING

Houthi attacks have employed a diverse and evolving arsenal. Anti-ship ballistic missiles, including the Burkan series and locally modified versions of Iranian Fateh missiles, have been the most challenging to intercept due to their high speed and maneuvering capability. Anti-ship cruise missiles, including variants of the Iranian Quds series, have been used extensively. One-way attack drones, modeled on Iranian Shahed designs, have been launched in salvos designed to overwhelm ship defenses. Armed surface drones have been deployed in swarm configurations. Several vessels have been struck and damaged, with at least two sunk. Crew members have been killed and injured in multiple incidents. The Houthis have demonstrated the ability to target specific vessels using commercial shipping databases and intelligence provided by Iranian assets.

SHIPPING INDUSTRY RESPONSE

The industry response to the Houthi threat has been dramatic and economically consequential. An estimated 80 to 90 percent of container shipping capacity that previously transited the Suez Canal has been rerouted to the Cape of Good Hope. This adds 10 to 14 days to Asia-Europe voyages, increases fuel consumption by approximately 30 to 40 percent per voyage, and has absorbed significant global container ship capacity, contributing to port congestion at alternative transshipment hubs. Container freight rates on the Europe-Asia route rose to their highest levels since the COVID-19 supply chain crisis. War risk insurance premiums for transits through the Red Sea have increased by factors of 10 to 20 compared to pre-crisis levels. Some specialized carriers, particularly those carrying non-Israeli-linked cargoes, have continued to transit with enhanced security measures, but the risk calculus has fundamentally changed.

MILITARY OPERATIONS AND LIMITATIONS

Operation Prosperity Guardian, the US-led naval coalition, has involved sustained air and naval strikes against Houthi missile and drone launch sites, radar installations, and command-and-control infrastructure across Yemen. The British Royal Navy has participated in strikes alongside American forces. Other coalition members have provided naval escorts. Hundreds of Houthi missiles and drones have been shot down by coalition warships, including through the use of SM-2, SM-6, and Tomahawk strikes on shore installations. Despite this significant military pressure, the Houthis have demonstrated a strategic resilience enabled by deep weapons caches, dispersal of capabilities, and Iranian resupply. The movement's political base among Yemeni Zaydi Shia populations has actually strengthened due to the attacks' resonance with the Gaza solidarity narrative.

INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT AND OUTLOOK

The Houthi shipping threat is assessed as persisting in its current form absent either a Gaza ceasefire that provides the movement a political off-ramp or a more fundamental degradation of its military infrastructure than has been achieved. The movement's strategic objectives — solidarity with Gaza, testing of Western deterrence, and asserting Yemeni agency — continue to be advanced by the campaign. Iran's calculation that the Houthi threat imposes significant economic and political costs on the West and Israel at low direct cost to Tehran makes continued Iranian support probable. The economic impact on Egypt through reduced Suez Canal revenues is a key second-order effect, creating pressures on an already fragile Egyptian economy. Global trade insurance and logistics patterns may take years to normalize even after hostilities cease.
