INTELLIGENCE REPORT: TAIWAN ELECTION RESULTS AND CHINA RESPONSE

CLASSIFICATION: POLITICAL AND MILITARY INTELLIGENCE REPORT
DATE: 2024-2025 ASSESSMENT
REGION: TAIWAN STRAIT, EAST ASIA

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Taiwan's presidential election of January 13, 2024 returned William Lai Ching-te of the Democratic Progressive Party to the presidency, marking the DPP's third consecutive presidential victory and extending its governance of Taiwan since 2016. Lai, who has a history of describing himself as a Taiwanese independence advocate, secured 40 percent of the vote in a three-way race, with the Kuomintang's Hou Yu-ih receiving 33 percent and the Taiwan People's Party's Ko Wen-je taking 26 percent. Beijing characterized Lai's election as a provocation and conducted military exercises around Taiwan in the weeks following the inauguration in May 2024. The election results have intensified the fundamental tension between Taiwan's democratic trajectory and Beijing's insistence on eventual unification under its terms.

ELECTION ANALYSIS AND POLITICAL DYNAMICS

Lai's victory, while decisive, reflected a divided electorate. The DPP lost its legislative majority, with the Kuomintang and Taiwan People's Party together holding more seats in the Legislative Yuan. This divided government dynamic constrains Lai's ability to pursue aggressive policy changes but does not fundamentally alter Taiwan's strategic posture. Voter demographics showed younger Taiwanese voters particularly resistant to unification narratives and strongly supportive of Taiwan's distinct democratic identity. Economic concerns about cross-strait trade dependence created some cross-cutting pressure. The KMT's relatively strong showing reflects continued support for engagement with the mainland, particularly among older voters and business communities, but unification as a near-term policy option commands minimal support across the political spectrum.

CHINESE RESPONSE AND MILITARY SIGNALING

Beijing's response to Lai's election included sustained military pressure through air and naval exercises. In May 2024, following Lai's inauguration, the People's Liberation Army conducted Joint Sword-2024A exercises featuring simultaneous operations in multiple maritime sectors around Taiwan, simulating a blockade scenario. Beijing refused to resume official cross-strait dialogue severed since 2016 and intensified United Front Work Department activities targeting Taiwanese businesses and civic organizations. Chinese diplomatic pressure successfully limited international congratulations to Lai from foreign governments. The PLA has continued regular air incursions into Taiwan's Air Defense Identification Zone, with record numbers of military aircraft participating in individual incursions.

US-TAIWAN RELATIONS AND ARMS SALES

The United States maintained its policy of strategic ambiguity while providing practical support to Taiwan's defense. Significant arms sales packages approved in 2024 included additional MIM-104 Patriot air defense system components, F-16 aircraft upgrades, and advanced munitions. The US Congress passed legislation strengthening Taiwan's defense cooperation framework and increasing funding for Taiwan security assistance. At the official level, the United States continued to insist it does not support Taiwan independence while opposing unilateral changes to the status quo. President Lai has managed cross-strait rhetoric carefully since taking office, moderating some of his pre-election statements to avoid unnecessary provocations while maintaining Taiwan's democratic distinctness.

INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT AND OUTLOOK

Taiwan's democratic consolidation and the entrenchment of a Taiwanese identity distinct from mainland Chinese identity represent long-term structural trends that complicate Beijing's unification goals. The PLA's military modernization continues to narrow Taiwan's defensive advantage window, making the 2027 to 2030 timeframe a period of elevated risk as Chinese military leadership has explicitly referenced as a readiness milestone. The most significant near-term variable is US policy clarity under the Trump administration, whose unpredictability has both reassured some allies with its toughness and alarmed others with potential transactionalism. Taiwan's own defense spending and mobilization reform progress are critical determinants of deterrence credibility. A cross-strait armed conflict remains a low-probability but catastrophic risk scenario warranting sustained intelligence prioritization.
