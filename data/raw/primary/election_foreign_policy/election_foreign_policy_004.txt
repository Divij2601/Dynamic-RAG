MACRON RE-ELECTED 2022: FRANCE AND THE PURSUIT OF EU STRATEGIC AUTONOMY

ELECTORAL CONTEXT AND SECOND-TERM MANDATE

Emmanuel Macron's re-election as French President in April 2022 was secured against Marine Le Pen of the Rassemblement National in a runoff vote that delivered Macron approximately 58 percent of the vote. While the margin was narrower than his 2017 victory, it represented a sufficient mandate for Macron to continue and deepen his signature foreign policy project: the construction of a more strategically autonomous European Union capable of acting independently of the United States when necessary.

The election took place against the backdrop of Russia's February 2022 invasion of Ukraine, which had transformed European security calculations overnight. Macron's pre-election shuttle diplomacy to Moscow and Kyiv, attempting to negotiate a ceasefire, attracted criticism from allies who argued he was legitimizing Putin, but also demonstrated his conviction that France and Europe could play an independent diplomatic role rather than simply deferring to Washington.

THE STRATEGIC AUTONOMY DOCTRINE

Macron had first articulated the concept of European strategic autonomy in a landmark 2017 Sorbonne address, but it was during his second term that the doctrine moved from aspiration toward practical implementation. Strategic autonomy for Macron meant Europe developing its own capacity to ensure security on its continent, exercise independent diplomatic initiatives, and make sovereign decisions on economic and technological matters without being perpetually constrained by American preferences or subject to American extraterritorial sanctions.

The concept encompassed military autonomy through the European Defence Fund, PESCO defense cooperation, and the Strategic Compass agreed in March 2022; technological autonomy through semiconductor investment programs, digital sovereignty initiatives, and the push to develop European alternatives to American cloud infrastructure; and diplomatic autonomy through French-led mediation efforts and resistance to what Paris called the "extraterritoriality" of US sanctions law.

UKRAINE WAR AND THE STRATEGIC AUTONOMY TEST

The war in Ukraine simultaneously strengthened and complicated Macron's strategic autonomy agenda. On one hand, it dramatically accelerated European defense investment and demonstrated the need for European security capacity. On the other hand, the immediate military crisis drove Europe closer to NATO and American leadership, as it was US intelligence, weapons systems, and financial guarantees that formed the backbone of Western support for Kyiv. European allies, particularly in Central and Eastern Europe, viewed Macron's continued back-channel communications with Moscow with suspicion, fearing they could undermine allied unity.

Macron's late 2023 statement that France had not ruled out sending Western ground troops to Ukraine broke significant taboos and was quickly walked back by German and American officials, illustrating both the ambitions and the limits of French strategic initiative within the alliance context.

EU GOVERNANCE AND MACRON'S EUROPEAN AGENDA

Beyond security, Macron's second term prioritized deepening EU governance. He championed reforms to EU fiscal rules, pushed for joint EU debt issuance to fund defense and green transition, and advocated for qualified majority voting on EU foreign policy to reduce the veto power of individual member states like Hungary. Progress on these institutional reforms was partial, as the consensus required for treaty change remained elusive.

France under Macron's second term also pursued an active Indo-Pacific strategy, articulating a distinctive French voice on Taiwan, regional security, and engagement with ASEAN nations. The 2021 AUKUS debacle, in which Australia canceled its French submarine contract in favor of a US-UK nuclear submarine arrangement, remained a sore point, but France worked to rebuild Indo-Pacific partnerships through bilateral agreements with India, Japan, and Pacific island states.

LEGACY AND CONSTRAINTS

Macron's second term was constrained by domestic political difficulties, including the loss of a parliamentary majority in June 2022 legislative elections. This forced him into political cohabitation dynamics that limited his domestic agenda while he continued to project France as a leading European voice on the world stage. His foreign policy legacy remains the establishment of strategic autonomy as the organizing concept of French and increasingly EU foreign policy thinking.
