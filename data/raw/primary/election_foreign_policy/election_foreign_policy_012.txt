MILEI'S ARGENTINA: LIBERTARIAN RADICAL FOREIGN POLICY TRANSFORMATION

THE 2023 ELECTION AND THE ANARCHO-CAPITALIST OUTSIDER

Javier Milei's victory in Argentina's November 2023 presidential runoff election against Peronist economy minister Sergio Massa represented one of the most ideologically radical political transitions in modern Latin American history. Milei, an economist and television personality who described himself as an "anarcho-capitalist," won 55.7 percent of the vote against Massa's 44.3 percent, riding a wave of fury at Argentina's chronic economic dysfunction: inflation above 140 percent annually, persistent poverty affecting over 40 percent of the population, and a sense of elite-driven policy failure spanning decades of Peronist and non-Peronist governments alike.

Milei's foreign policy platform was as radical as his domestic economic program. He explicitly positioned Argentina in alignment with the United States and Israel, and in sharp opposition to socialist and leftist governments in Latin America and beyond, calling the leaders of Venezuela, Cuba, Nicaragua, Brazil, Mexico, and Chile "communists" or their variants during and after the campaign.

REJECTION OF THE BRICS INVITATION

One of Milei's most consequential immediate foreign policy decisions was declining Argentina's BRICS membership. Argentina had been invited to join the expanded BRICS grouping alongside Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iran, Ethiopia, and Egypt, an invitation the outgoing Peronist government had accepted. Milei reversed this decision upon taking office, explicitly citing his opposition to aligning Argentina with governments he considered authoritarian and his desire to align the country more closely with Western democratic institutions and market economies. The decision signaled a fundamental reorientation away from the South-South cooperation framework that had characterized Kirchnerist foreign policy.

ALIGNMENT WITH THE UNITED STATES AND ISRAEL

Milei pursued a conspicuous alignment with the United States and Israel that was unusual even by the standards of right-wing Latin American governments. He visited Washington early in his tenure, expressed personal admiration for Trump and for aspects of Israeli policy, and positioned Argentina as a reliable partner for Western interests. He traveled to Israel during the Gaza conflict and expressed strong support for Israeli security operations, a position that isolated Argentina within the region and within the broader Global South.

The relationship with the Trump administration, which took office in January 2025, was notably warm. Milei and Trump shared ideological affinity and personal rapport. Argentina supported several US positions in international forums that Latin American neighbors opposed, and the Trump administration provided diplomatic backing for Argentina's IMF negotiations.

IMF RELATIONS AND ECONOMIC DIPLOMACY

Argentina's foreign policy under Milei was inseparable from its acute economic crisis and its relationship with the International Monetary Fund. Argentina carried the IMF's largest ever loan obligation, and Milei's entire economic project required continuous IMF goodwill and disbursements. The government's aggressive fiscal adjustment, dollarization debates, and regulatory dismantlement were partly designed to satisfy IMF and bond market requirements. US support for Argentina's IMF programs — Washington holds decisive influence at the fund — was therefore a material foreign policy objective that reinforced the ideological alignment with Washington.

REGIONAL ISOLATION AND MERCOSUR TENSIONS

Milei's combative personal style and ideological militancy created frictions with virtually every neighbor. His public attacks on Brazilian President Lula, Mexican President Lopez Obrador (and his successor Sheinbaum), and Chilean President Boric generated sustained regional tensions. Argentina's relationships within MERCOSUR became strained, with Brazil and other members resisting Argentine pressure to accelerate the MERCOSUR-EU trade agreement on terms that Argentina demanded. Milei's foreign policy model was oriented away from regional multilateralism and toward bilateral alignment with the Anglophone world.

Despite the controversies, Milei's foreign policy reflected a coherent if radical ideological vision: that Argentina's interests were best served by alignment with liberal market democracies rather than solidarity with the Global South.
