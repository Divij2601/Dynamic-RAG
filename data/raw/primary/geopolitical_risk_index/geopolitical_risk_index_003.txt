IRAN GEOPOLITICAL RISK SCORE: NUCLEAR AND MILITARY DIMENSIONS

OVERVIEW

Iran's geopolitical risk profile in 2025 is shaped by three interlocking threat dimensions: its nuclear program's advancement toward weapons capability, its support for regional proxy militias across the Middle East, and its domestic political instability following mass protests and economic deterioration. Iran ranks in the top decile globally for conflict-generation risk, serving as the primary state sponsor of destabilizing non-state actors from Lebanon to Yemen to Iraq. The death of President Ebrahim Raisi in a helicopter crash in May 2024 and the subsequent election of reformist Masoud Pezeshkian introduced uncertainty into Iran's political trajectory, though Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei retains ultimate authority over all strategic decisions including the nuclear file.

NUCLEAR PROGRAM STATUS AND RISK

Iran's nuclear program represents the central risk variable for Middle East regional stability. By 2025, Iran has enriched uranium to 60 percent purity — a level with no civilian justification — and possesses sufficient enriched uranium to produce multiple nuclear weapons if further enriched to weapons-grade (90 percent). The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) has reported that Iran has progressively limited inspector access and removed surveillance cameras, severely degrading international monitoring. The 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) collapsed following the U.S. withdrawal under the Trump administration in 2018 and has not been revived despite multiple negotiating rounds. Israeli intelligence assessments indicate Iran could achieve nuclear breakout capability (sufficient fissile material for one weapon) within weeks to months under current enrichment trajectories. A nuclear-armed Iran would fundamentally alter Middle East deterrence dynamics and trigger Saudi Arabian and Turkish nuclear hedging.

REGIONAL PROXY NETWORK AND "AXIS OF RESISTANCE"

Iran's most effective strategic instrument is its network of proxy and partner armed groups, collectively termed the "Axis of Resistance." Hezbollah in Lebanon represents Iran's most capable proxy, with an arsenal exceeding 150,000 rockets and missiles. Hamas in Gaza received Iranian funding and weapons prior to the October 7, 2023 attack on Israel. The Houthi movement (Ansar Allah) in Yemen has conducted unprecedented Red Sea shipping disruptions, attacking commercial vessels and drawing U.S. and allied military responses throughout 2024-2025. Iraqi Shia militias organized under the Popular Mobilization Forces have attacked U.S. bases repeatedly. This network provides Iran with strategic depth and plausible deniability while imposing costs on adversaries across multiple theaters simultaneously.

DOMESTIC POLITICAL RISK

The "Woman, Life, Freedom" protests of 2022-2023 represented the most significant challenge to the Islamic Republic since its founding, triggered by the morality police killing of Mahsa Amini. The regime suppressed protests with lethal force, executing several demonstrators. Economic grievances remain acute: inflation exceeds 40 percent, the rial has lost over 80 percent of its value against the dollar since sanctions were reimposed, and youth unemployment is structurally elevated. The Revolutionary Guards (IRGC) have expanded their economic role, controlling major industries, creating a security-economic complex resistant to reform. Pezeshkian's reformist credentials are constrained by Khamenei's veto over strategic policy, limiting the reform potential of his presidency.

ISRAEL-IRAN DIRECT CONFRONTATION RISK

April 2024 marked a historic threshold: Iran conducted its first direct missile and drone attack on Israeli territory (over 300 projectiles), responding to an Israeli strike on Iranian diplomatic premises in Damascus. Israel retaliated with a strike on Iranian air defense systems near Isfahan. This direct exchange, while both parties exercised escalation restraint, established a new precedent of direct bilateral military action and creates a risk of escalation in future cycles. Israeli military planners have openly discussed preemptive strike scenarios against Iranian nuclear infrastructure, which would risk a regional war involving Hezbollah, Iraqi militias, and potentially triggering Houthi escalation.

COMPOSITE RISK SCORE

Iran's composite geopolitical risk score is VERY HIGH, with nuclear proliferation risk categorized as the most consequential medium-term regional threat. The combination of nuclear advancement, proxy warfare capacity, and internal instability makes Iran the defining risk variable for Middle East geopolitical stability through 2027.
